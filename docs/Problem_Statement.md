# Problem Statement

Conventional hydrological models are computationally expensive, require highly specialized geological inputs, and fail to provide immediate, actionable warnings. Key limitations of traditional approaches include:

1. **High Complexity**: Physics-based models require massive datasets representing underground soil profiles and complex topographic contours, which are rarely available in real-time.
2. **Lack of Model Adaptability**: Many existing flood systems rely on a single, static algorithmic model, failing to automatically evaluate and adapt to fresh training data.
3. **No Interactive Usability**: High-accuracy forecasts are often locked behind terminal scripts or command-line outputs, preventing disaster control officers from performing instant calculations on the field.

**Rising Waters** addresses these issues by delivering:
- A streamlined machine learning classifier pipeline that runs in seconds.
- An automated model selection engine that selects the best algorithm.
- A fully interactive, responsive web portal equipped with form validation and downloadable PDF reports.
